6 April 2024

Suicide Bluffs

  • Country

    Canada

  • Location

    Mt Seymour Provincial Park

  • Activity

    Day walk

Before some Seymour snowboarding, Cas & I decided to explore the area west of the ski field with a short hike. We snagged some microspikes from Guest Services & set off towards Dog Mountain. We were glad we had the spikes, as the trail, despite recent snow, was packed & icy. The hike was quick & straightforward, with no technical challenges or elevation gain. We paused at the lookout to enjoy a muesli bar & identified some birds.

For our return, we wanted more of a challenge, so we scoped out Suicide Bluffs. Although the trail is marked on the topo map, there were no footprints & few markers. Luckily, the steep cliff to our left provided a clear guide of where not to go. While the Dog Mountain trail had been well-packed, the path to Suicide Bluffs had deep snow, post-holing each step. After a steep, slippery, snowy slog, we reached the top, but since leaving Dog Mountain clouds & snow had come though. We'll have to return on a clearer day for that view.

We made our way back through knee-deep snow to Mt. Seymour trail junction. After returning our microspikes & heating up a toastie at Guest Services, we headed out for some boarding. Unfortunately, the Mystery Chairlift stopped just two rows from the front, & after a 30-minute wait, we were about to head home when it finally resumed. We managed to squeeze in a few good runs before heading home for dinner.
